Peaks

June 07, 2020
Philosophy Econ

Coins clanked rhythmically in his pockets as he hurried down the wet sidewalk, a feeble umbrella held tightly in his shaking hand, towards the fog-tipped peaks in the distance. The downpour never ceased as he passed a donut shop, powdered sugar interspersed with shards of glass covering the linoleum-tiled shelves; a parking garage, empty but for a lone motorcycle; a subway station boarded up with dusty wooden planks ravaged by termites and age. His jacket tore and dimes, nickels, pennies poured out of his pockets and onto the wet sidewalk. A gust of wind removed the withered umbrella from his outstretched hand like a balloon from a young child. He looked down at the cracked, wet sidewalk below the ragged tips of his black shoes that kept moving forward. Pulling his torn jacket closer around him, he continued moving forward, against the rain, towards the fog-tipped peaks, the moon a shining nickel behind the money-green trees that dotted the distant hills. He shivered.

He lay in bed under the ceiling of a room with white walls and gray curtains. A monitor with a flat green line sat idly next to him. A flat, money-green line. No peaks.
