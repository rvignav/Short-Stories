Lemonade

June 17, 2019

Removing her headphones from her ears and placing them in her backpack, she paused the string orchestral rendition of Beethoven’s Moonlight Sonata playing on her iPod and made her way towards the lemonade sand sitting in the shade of a fir tree at the corner of the road. Fishing for coins in her cluttered back pockets, she eventually placed three dollar coins on the table before her and ordered a lemonade; much to her surprise, however, the clerk responded, “Unfortunately, we’re all out of lemons. Can I interest you in a berry smoothie?”

Scanning over the stand, she saw an assortment of fruits—oranges, strawberries, blueberries, plums, mangos, kiwis, and even fresh lychees. Yet she still grabbed the coins from the table, nodded no, and continued her afternoon jog, the soles of her shoes wearily continuing forward as the sun’s heat continued to warm the bottle of ice-cold lemonade that lay forgotten in her backpack.
